LeanHRPT currently supports the following satellites and transmission modes;

POES (NOAA)

HRPT - High Resolution Picture Transmission

Both LeanHRPT-Demod and Decode fully support decoding the HRPT broadcasts from the 5th generation NOAA POES weather satellites (NOAA-15 through 19). Input formats supported by LeanHRPT-Decode are raw demodulated data (.bin), the .raw16 format used by other software, as well as .hrp files distributed by NOAA and others.

GAC - Global Area Coverage

Usually after completing each orbit, a NOAA POES satellite will play back its stored data to one of the NOAA (and affiliated) ground stations. This transmission is done either using one of the spare L-band transmitters (1698, 1702.5, 1707 MHz) or the sole S-band transmitter (2247.5 MHz), at a higher rate than standard HRPT.

If you are in range of these short broadcasts, you can use LeanHRPT to demodulate and decode the transmissions to obtain full orbit imagery (albeit at a reduced resolution). GAC files produced by LeanHRPT-Demod (and accepted by LeanHRPT-Decode) are raw demodulated data (.bin).

Meteor-M

HRPT - High Resolution Picture Transmission

LeanHRPT processes Meteor-M HRPT files in the raw demodulated form (.bin). LeanHRPT-Decode will also accept deframed Meteor-M HRPT files.

LRPT - Low Resolution Picture Transmission

LeanHRPT-Decode supports processing Meteor-M LRPT files in .cadu format. The latest release version of LeanHRPT-Demod does not have the capability to demodulate LRPT as of yet.

MetOp

HRPT - High Resolution Picture Transmission

Both LeanHRPT-Demod and Decode support processing MetOp HRPT in the .vcdu format. Additionally, LeanHRPT-Decode can also accept MetOp HRPT .cadu files.

FengYun-3

HRPT (L-band) - High Resolution Picture Transmission

Support for FengYun-3 files is identical to MetOp, meaning that LeanHRPT works with .vcdu files but will accept .cadu files. Only L-band HRPT files from FengYun-3B and 3C are supported.
